## 🎯 Project Management Process Summary

OctoAcme operates on a **customer-first, iterative delivery model** with clear ownership and data-informed decision-making. The project lifecycle follows a structured five-phase approach:

1. **Initiation** — Problem validation and stakeholder alignment through a lightweight One-pager
2. **Planning** — Scope definition, backlog creation, and dependency mapping
3. **Execution** — Build, test, review, and iterate with regular standups and tracking
4. **Release** — Staged deployment with pre-release verification and rollback plans
5. **Close & Retrospective** — Learning capture and continuous process improvement

### Core Principles

- **Customer-first:** Prioritize customer value and usability in all decisions
- **Iterative delivery:** Deliver small, testable increments with regular feedback loops
- **Clear ownership:** Each project has named Project Managers and Product Managers
- **Data-informed:** Measure impact and iterate based on evidence and metrics
- **Psychological safety:** Encourage feedback, learning, and blameless retrospectives

### Key Roles

- **Project Managers** — Coordinate delivery, manage timelines, risks, and stakeholder communication
- **Product Managers** — Define what to build, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes
- **Developers** — Implement features with quality, testability, and maintainability
- **QA/Testing** — Validate quality and acceptance criteria
- **Stakeholders** — Provide inputs, approvals, and business context

### Communication & Execution

- **Daily standups** (15 min) — Focus on progress, blockers, and dependencies
- **Weekly PM/PdM syncs** — Alignment and risk management
- **Twice-weekly team standups** — Delivery cadence and adjustments
- **Monthly stakeholder updates** — Progress and strategic alignment
- **Small, shippable increments** — PRs ≤400 lines with clear acceptance criteria
- **Embedded quality** — Unit tests, integration tests, security scanning in CI, smoke tests pre-release

### Continuous Improvement

- **Post-sprint retrospectives** — Capture learnings and identify action items
- **Risk management** — Maintain risk register with clear owners and mitigation plans
- **Escalation paths** — Team → PM → Product Lead → Sponsor for blocked issues
- **Measurement culture** — Track velocity, burndown, success metrics, and operational signals

## 🚀 Getting Started

**For new team members:**
1. Start with [Project Management Overview](octoacme-project-management-overview.md) for a high-level understanding
2. Review [Roles & Personas](octoacme-roles-and-personas.md) to understand your role and responsibilities
3. Explore the phase-specific guides (Initiation → Planning → Execution → Release) as relevant to your project

**For project leads:**
1. Use [Project Initiation Guide](octoacme-project-initiation.md) to kick off new work
2. Reference [Project Planning](octoacme-project-planning.md) for scope and backlog setup
3. Maintain [Risk Management & Communication](octoacme-risks-and-communication.md) artifacts throughout the project lifecycle

**For delivery teams:**
1. Align on [Execution & Tracking](octoacme-execution-and-tracking.md) workflows and quality standards
2. Refer to [Release & Deployment Guide](octoacme-release-and-deployment.md) before releasing to production
3. Participate in [Retrospective & Continuous Improvement](octoacme-retrospective-and-continuous-improvement.md) sessions
